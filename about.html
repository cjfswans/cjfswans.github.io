Biography

Brief Bio

I was born and raised in Racine in Southeastern Wisconsin. My relationship with computing dates to the Apple II I used as a child and was developed throughout the 90s by countless hours surfing Web 1.0 and endlessly taking apart my parents’ computers. Although I have always loved computers and technology, I had never been exposed to Computer Science in my primary or secondary educations, so did not really give it much consideration when I went to attend Harvard College, where I instead studied Government with a focus on political philosophy. After college, I went on to the University of Chicago’s Master of Arts Program in the Humanities, focusing on cinema and media studies. For much of my post-collegiate life, I have been teaching in a wide variety of capacities – first as a K-12 substitute teacher in Racine public schools, later as an adjunct instructor in Philosophy and a full-time preschool teacher, and finally at the University of Texas at Austin, where I taught in the Department of Rhetoric and Writing while working on a PhD in American Studies. It was in Austin that I began to become more familiar with the technology sector, and ultimately I decided after a year there to return to Racine to begin my career change in earnest.
